Bitcoin, Dogecoin, Ethereum

What it is all about?

Let's start with the big one. Bitcoin. Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency, that can be sent on the peer-to-peer network. The cryptocurrency was invented in 2008 by an unknown person or group of people using the name Satoshi Nakamoto and began use in 2009 when its implementation was released as an open-source project.

So what's so special about it?

Well, it is mostly thanks to something called a blockchain. Another weird name? Yes, but it's not very complicated. Actually, it is but let me explain in few words. Blockchain is a public database that records transactions that are stored in chained blocks. Ethereum?

Ethereum is not actually a cryptocurrency, it is an open-source blockchain. However, it has a native currency called Ether that is used in transactions and as a "gas fee". Ethereum is after Bitcoin second-largest crypto on the market. Its price has very close relations with the number one currency. The chart below shows the price over time of both assets.

What about Dogecoin?

Dogecoin is the little brother of Bitcoin. Or more like a cousin, you didn't know existed until he became famous because of a joke. Dogecoin is a cryptocurrency with a face of Shiba Inu dog created as a joke. It was introduced on December 6, 2013, but only recently, at the end of 2020 started gaining attention. As a result of the TikTok trend, Reddit users and Elon Musk price surged to 0.07$ in January 2021. Currently, memecoin is in the top 10 of the total market capitalization of crypto assets.

How crypto stands comapred to other assets?
